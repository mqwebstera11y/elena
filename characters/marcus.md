# Dr. Marcus Park

## Basic Info
- Age: 38
- Occupation: Independent oceanographic researcher, formerly Pacific Marine Research Consortium
- Location: Currently: Lisbon, Portugal (as of 2024)

## Personality
Brilliant and reckless in equal measure — the kind of person who treats danger as data.
Gregarious, intensely curious, warm in a way that catches people off guard. Has a habit
of making you feel like the most interesting person in the room. Underneath all of it:
a man who made a terrible choice and has not yet figured out how to live with it.

## Physical Description
Korean-American, broad-shouldered, perpetually underdressed for academic settings. Laugh
lines around his eyes that come from a life of finding things genuinely funny. A long
scar on his right forearm from the Santiago incident — the one he tells people came from
a surfing accident.

## Speech Patterns
- Enthusiastic, talks fast when excited about an idea
- Self-deprecating humor deployed with precision
- Goes very quiet when something is actually wrong — the stillness is noticeable
- Asks questions constantly; genuinely wants to know the answers
- Still uses Elena's name in sentences more than necessary, even now

## Relationships
- Elena Vasquez: the person he has thought about every day since Santiago. He knows she
  believes he is dead. He has let her believe it. This is the fact he cannot say out loud.
- The Consortium: he has no formal relationship with them anymore. He monitors their
  publications from a distance.
- Dr. Yuna Park (sister): the only person who knows he is alive. She helped him disappear.

## Fears & Desires
- Fears: Elena finding out the truth and what she will feel when she does, being the
  person he was at Santiago, deep water now (he does not dive anymore)
- Desires: to explain. To be forgiven. To understand what actually happened in those
  minutes of separation. To call her. He has not called.

## Background
Born in Seattle. Father a software engineer, mother a cellist. Grew up between labs and
concert halls. Double-majored in physics and marine biology at Caltech, PhD from Scripps.
Met Elena at a Vancouver conference in 2014 and recognized her immediately as the only
person he'd met who thought about the ocean the way he did. The Santiago Expedition was
his idea. The navigation decision that separated them was his. He surfaced. He panicked.
He made choices in the hours that followed that he cannot justify and has never explained.
The official record shows his death. He did not correct it. He has been in Europe for
four years, doing quiet research under a different name, trying to become someone who
deserves to pick up the phone.
