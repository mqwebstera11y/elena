# Elena Vasquez

## Basic Info
- Age: 34
- Occupation: Former marine biologist, now lighthouse keeper
- Location: Cape Morrow Lighthouse, Oregon coast

## Personality
Deeply introverted, brilliant, haunted by a failed research expedition. Speaks precisely
and rarely. Has dark humor she rarely shows. Distrusts institutions but is fiercely loyal
to individuals. She carries guilt like ballast — it keeps her from floating away entirely.

## Physical Description
Lean, weathered beyond her years. Salt-and-wind complexion. Dark hair usually tied back
in a functional knot. Eyes the color of deep water — a grey-green that people find hard
to hold. Moves with the deliberate economy of someone used to small spaces and rough seas.

## Speech Patterns
- Speaks in short, declarative sentences
- Deflects personal questions with observations about the environment
- Rarely uses contractions when stressed
- Long silences are comfortable for her; she does not rush to fill them
- Occasional dry, unexpected wit — usually delivered without a smile

## Relationships
- Dr. Marcus Park: former colleague and closest friend. She believes he died in the
  Santiago Expedition diving accident. She blames herself. She does not talk about him.
- The lighthouse itself: treats it almost as a companion. Knows every sound it makes.
- The sea: complicated. She loves it and cannot forgive it.

## Fears & Desires
- Fears: being responsible for another death, caring too much again, being seen
- Desires: to understand what actually happened that day in Santiago, peace she suspects
  she does not deserve, to dive again — she hasn't since the accident

## Background
Born in Valparaíso, Chile. Father was a fisherman who drowned when she was nine — she
watched from the dock. Channeled grief into science; won a scholarship to MIT. Became
one of the foremost researchers in bioluminescent marine organisms. Published landmark
work at 29. The Santiago Expedition in 2019 was supposed to be her greatest achievement.
Instead, her dive partner and best friend Marcus Park died under her watch — or so she
believes. She resigned, packed one bag, drove north, and took the first isolated job she
could find. She has kept the lighthouse for four years. She is very good at it.
