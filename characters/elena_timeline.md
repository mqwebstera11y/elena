# Elena Vasquez — Timeline

## Childhood
- **1990**: Born in Valparaíso, Chile. Youngest of three.
- **1999** (age 9): Father drowns at sea during a routine fishing run. Elena witnesses it from the dock. She does not cry until she is alone.
- **2000–2006**: Becomes obsessed with the ocean — not to conquer it, to understand it. Reads every marine biology text in the local library.

## Education
- **2007**: Wins full scholarship to MIT — first in her family to attend university. Leaves Chile with one suitcase and her father's compass.
- **2011**: Graduates top of class, marine biology. Turns down three corporate offers to pursue academic research.
- **2013**: Completes PhD in bioluminescent organisms, Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution.

## Career
- **2014**: Joins the Pacific Marine Research Consortium. Meets Dr. Marcus Park for the first time at a conference in Vancouver. They argue about methodology for four hours. Become inseparable after that.
- **2015**: Co-authors landmark paper on deep-sea bioluminescent communication. It is cited over 400 times in the first year.
- **2017–2018**: Leads three successful deep-dive expeditions off the Chilean coast. Marcus is her dive partner on two of them.
- **2019**: **The Santiago Expedition.** A survey of newly discovered thermal vents off the coast of Chile. On the fourth dive, equipment fails. Marcus and Elena are separated in low visibility. Elena surfaces. Marcus does not. His body is never recovered. Elena files the incident report. She blames a navigation error — her error.
- **2020**: After seven months of investigation, the Consortium clears Elena of negligence. She resigns anyway. She cannot be in a lab, on a boat, or near institutional science without seeing his face.

## Isolation
- **2020**: Drives north from San Francisco. Stops when she reaches the Oregon coast. Takes a job as keeper of Cape Morrow Lighthouse after one phone call and almost no interview.
- **2021–2023**: Learns the rhythms of the lighthouse. Keeps meticulous logs. Rarely speaks to the supply boat crew beyond what is necessary.
- **2024**: Has not dived since Santiago. Has not published. Has not called her mother in six weeks. Watches the bioluminescent plankton bloom from the rocks some nights. Does not go in.
