# Dr. Marcus Park — Timeline

## Childhood & Education
- **1986**: Born in Seattle, Washington. Father: software engineer. Mother: cellist.
- **2004**: Enters Caltech. Double-majors in physics and marine biology — advised against by three separate professors.
- **2008**: Graduates with honors. Immediately begins PhD program at Scripps Institution of Oceanography.
- **2011**: Completes PhD. Joins Pacific Marine Research Consortium.

## Career (Pre-Santiago)
- **2014**: Meets Elena Vasquez at a conference in Vancouver. They argue for four hours about deep-sea survey methodology. He thinks she is the most rigorous person he has ever met. He considers this a compliment of the highest order.
- **2015**: Co-authors the bioluminescent communication paper with Elena. It becomes one of the most cited papers of the decade. He is proud of it but knows it is mostly her work.
- **2017–2018**: Dive partner on two of Elena's Chilean coast expeditions. He is reckless in the water and she is not, and they balance each other.
- **2019 (January)**: Proposes the Santiago Expedition to the Consortium. The thermal vent discovery is his lead. He assembles the team. Elena is his first call.

## The Santiago Incident (2019)
- **April 14, 2019**: Fourth dive of the Santiago Expedition. Visibility drops unexpectedly near the thermal vent. Marcus and Elena become separated. His equipment shows a navigation error — **his** navigation error, not hers, though the logs as filed do not reflect this.
- **April 14, 2019 (surface)**: Marcus surfaces forty minutes after separation. He is disoriented, hypothermic, and alone. He waits. Elena surfaces twenty minutes later. She has been searching for him.
- **April 14–16, 2019**: In the chaos of the incident report and the Consortium response, Marcus makes a decision he has never fully explained to himself: he does not correct Elena's assumption about who caused the navigation failure. Then, in a moment of panic and shame he still cannot reconstruct clearly, he disappears from the hospital before Elena can reach him. The Consortium, believing him dead or lost at sea, files accordingly.
- **May 2019**: Marcus contacts his sister Yuna from Lisbon. She is the only person who knows. She does not approve. She helps him anyway.

## Aftermath
- **2019–2020**: Establishes a quiet research identity in Portugal under a modified name (M. Park). Publishes small papers in European journals on thermal vent ecosystems — he cannot stop doing the work.
- **2021**: Learns through Yuna that Elena has become a lighthouse keeper in Oregon. He does not know what to do with this information. He thinks about it constantly.
- **2022**: Begins drafting a letter to Elena. Has written forty-three versions. Has sent none.
- **2024**: Still in Lisbon. Still researching. Still not diving. The scar on his arm has faded but not gone. He checks the Cape Morrow weather reports sometimes, the way you check on something you don't have the right to check on.
